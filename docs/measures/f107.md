# F10.7 — 10.7 cm solar radio flux

## Physical meaning

F10.7 is the integrated flux density of solar radio emission at
**2 800 MHz** (10.7 cm wavelength), measured at the top of Earth's
atmosphere. Most of this signal arises from the chromosphere and lower
corona above sunspot regions, so F10.7 tracks photospheric magnetic
activity *and* the overlying gas above it. It correlates closely with
SSN but is a continuous, calibrated, instrumentally-traceable number —
not a count — which is why aeronomy / atmospheric drag models prefer it.

## Source

- **Primary instrument:** Dominion Radio Astrophysical Observatory (DRAO),
  Penticton, BC, Canada. Operated since 1947 (continuous since 1966).
- **Aggregator used by `sunspot`:**
  - NASA GSFC / SPDF **OMNI2** hourly flat files (`omni2_{year}.dat`),
    flagged column `f107`.
  - NOAA SWPC text product `Daily_Solar_Indices.txt` is also parsed (for
    the most recent days the OMNI2 file may not yet cover).
- **Units:** `sfu` (solar flux units), where
  1 sfu = 10⁻²² W m⁻² Hz⁻¹.

## Cadence and resampling

- **OMNI2 native cadence:** hourly. `load_omni2_daily` arithmetic-means each
  UTC day (per OMNI's daily-average definition).
- **NOAA SWPC native cadence:** already daily.
- **Sunspot internal cadence:** daily, reindexed to the analysis window in
  `sunspot.align.join.to_daily_dataframe`.

## Range, sentinels, gaps

- **Typical range:** ~ 65 sfu (deep solar minimum) to ~ 300 sfu (cycle peak).
  Daily flares can briefly push values higher.
- **Missing-value sentinel:** OMNI2 uses `999.9` (float `999.9`) for missing
  hourly F10.7. The parser converts these to `NaN`.
- **Gaps:** Sub-daily gaps in OMNI2 are uncommon since 1966; intermittent
  gaps before 1966 are larger.

## Pipeline use

- Default second metric in `--metrics ssn,f107,dst,ap`.
- Used in `save_commits_solar_dynamics` (z(F10.7) overlay alongside z(SSN)).
- Cross-correlation, lag scan, MI lag, and per-window MA correlation curves
  all run identically against F10.7.

## Pitfalls

- **F10.7 vs F10.7-adjusted.** OMNI2 ships the *observed* flux (Earth-Sun
  distance variations included). Some aeronomy models use the
  *adjusted* (1-AU) flux, ~3 % different over the year. `sunspot` reports
  the observed value; do not mix them in long-baseline trend work.
- **Saturated flares.** Single hours can spike to thousands of sfu during X-
  or M-class flares; the daily mean dampens this but extreme tails remain.
